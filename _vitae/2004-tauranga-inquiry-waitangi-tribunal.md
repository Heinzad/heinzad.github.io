*Advise a Commission of Inquiry*

Role: Research Analyst & Inquiry Facilitator.  
Tauranga Moana District Inquiry Stage 2  
Waitangi Tribunal Unit, Ministry of Justice.  

Outcome:  

- [Tauranga Moana 1886-2006: Report on the Post-Raupatu Claims](https://forms.justice.govt.nz/search/Documents/WT/wt_DOC_68361885/Wai215vol1.pdf), Waitangi Tribunal, Wellington, 2010.  

---

Facilitated a commission of inquiry into 60 contemporary claims remaining after the Waitangi Tribunal had completed its report on the Nineteenth Century, from the first consultation and interlocutory conferences, and a research programme, through to the end of hearings in 2006, and reporting in 2007.  

- Co-ordinated consultative and interlocutory conferences, joint statement of issues and hearings.
- Advised presiding officer and Tribunal members, drafted Tribunal memoranda.
- Developed relationships with tangata whenua, legal representatives, and external stakeholders.
- Co-ordinated research programme, arranged assessment of research needs, drafted project briefs and commissions, engaged and liaised with external contractors, co-ordinated QA of research reports.
- Developed and maintained inquiry web page and document store. 
  
Responsibilities:  

- Consult iwi, hapu and whanau to develop research programmes. 
- Advise a Commission of Inquiry.
